\subsection{3D Printing Custom-Made PLCL\label{sec:discussion:3dPrintingPLCL:customPLCL}}

Adjustments from a hardware (nozzle type) and software (printing temperature) perspective were made to address the repeated clogging the custom-made PLCL would cause in the printer hotend.

Because all changes to the printer itself did not resolve the clogging, it is likely that the custom-made filament is the contributing factor. Although the custom-made PLCL filament had an average diameter of $1.75mm$, the variability was outside the ideal bounds of Â±$0.1mm$. It is possible this high tolerance contributed to clogging, and research should be conducted to create custom-made PLCL with a narrower margin of filament thickness.

Additionally, a procedure for cleaning hotend clogs should be established to quickly and efficiently remove clogs in the nozzle.
